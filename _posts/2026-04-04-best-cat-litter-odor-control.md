## Buying Guide

Choosing the right cat litter for odor control can feel like a big decision, especially with so many factors at play. Here are some practical tips to help you make an informed choice that works for both you and your feline companion.

### 1. Litter Type and Material

Different litter materials offer varying levels of odor control. Clay litters, like bentonite clay, are excellent at absorbing moisture and clumping, which helps trap odors. Silica gel litters are highly absorbent and can last longer between changes, making them great for odor control. Natural litters made from pine, corn, or wheat are biodegradable and can be good for odor control, though their clumping ability might vary. Consider your cat's preferences too; some cats prefer the texture of clay, while others are fine with natural options.

### 2. Clumping Ability

For effective cat litter odor control, strong clumping is key. When litter clumps tightly around urine and feces, it seals in the odors and makes it easy to scoop out the waste completely. This prevents contaminated litter from lingering in the box and continuously releasing unpleasant smells. Look for litters that explicitly state "hard clumping" or "strong clumping" on their packaging. A litter that forms loose, crumbly clumps will ultimately lead to more lingering odor.

### 3. Scented vs. Unscented

This is a personal preference for both you and your cat. Scented litters, often infused with freshness boosters like Febreze or baking soda, can provide an extra layer of odor neutralization. However, some cats (and people!) are sensitive to strong fragrances, which can deter them from using the litter box. If your cat seems hesitant to use a scented litter, or if you have allergies, an unscented formula with excellent natural odor-trapping abilities might be a better choice. Many unscented options, like Dr. Elsey's, still excel at cat litter odor control.

### 4. Dust Level

A dusty litter isn't just annoying; it can contribute to respiratory issues for both you and your cat. Fine dust particles can carry odors, and they certainly don't make for a pleasant scooping experience. Look for litters marketed as "low dust" or "99% dust-free." While no litter is truly 100% dust-free, some brands do a much better job than others at minimizing airborne particles. A cleaner litter means cleaner air and less irritation for everyone. Remember, proper litter box hygiene goes hand-in-hand with good litter — and don't forget to provide other essentials like a sturdy [cat scratching posts](/pawpicks/pet-accessories/best-cat-scratching-posts/) to keep your cat happy and healthy!

### 5. Multi-Cat Formulas

If you share your home with more than one cat, opting for a multi-cat formula is almost always a good idea. These litters are specifically engineered with enhanced odor-fighting power to handle the increased waste and odor output from multiple felines. They often feature stronger clumping action and more robust odor neutralizers to keep pace with the demands of a busy litter box.
