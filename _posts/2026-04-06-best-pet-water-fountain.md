## What Could Be Better

While the [PetSafe Drinkwell Platinum Pet Fountain 168oz](https://amzn.to/41dtOOM) offers many benefits, there are a few areas where we feel it could be improved, particularly for a product designed for long-term daily use.

**Cleaning Can Be a Bit Fiddly:** Although PetSafe states that most parts are dishwasher safe (excluding the pump), we found the design has several nooks, crannies, and tight spaces that tend to accumulate grime, especially around the pump housing and inside the water channels. Over time, a biofilm can develop, and if not thoroughly cleaned, it can become a breeding ground for bacteria or even algae in warmer environments. While we appreciate the dishwasher-safe components, we still found ourselves needing to use a small brush (often sold separately) to manually scrub these harder-to-reach areas during our weekly deep clean. This adds a bit more effort to the maintenance routine than we'd ideally like.

**Ongoing Filter Replacement Costs:** The effectiveness of the [PetSafe Drinkwell Platinum Pet Fountain 168oz](https://amzn.to/41dtOOM)'s filtration system is excellent, but it comes with a recurring cost: filter replacements. Both the carbon filters and pre-filters need to be changed regularly – typically every 2-4 weeks for the carbon filter, depending on water quality and the number of pets using the fountain. While the initial purchase price of the fountain is budget-friendly, these ongoing filter expenses can add up over a year. It's an essential part of keeping the water fresh and the pump running smoothly, but it's something potential buyers should factor into their budget planning. You can save a bit by buying filters in bulk, but it's still a continuous outlay.

**Plastic Construction:** The [PetSafe Drinkwell Platinum Pet Fountain 168oz](https://amzn.to/41dtOOM) is made from BPA-free plastic, which keeps the cost down and makes it lightweight. However, plastic can be more prone to scratching over time compared to stainless steel or ceramic options. These scratches can potentially harbor bacteria, making thorough cleaning even more critical. Additionally, some pets, especially those with chin acne (a common issue in cats), might react better to non-plastic bowls. While we appreciate the affordability and durability of the plastic, pet owners who prioritize materials like stainless steel for their non-porous nature and ease of sterilization might find this a drawback. It's a trade-off for the lower price point.
