The [PetSafe Easy Walk No-Pull Dog Harness XL](https://amzn.to/4coIuRk) is a popular and widely recommended walking solution designed to help owners manage dogs that pull on the leash. When your dog tries to pull, the leash attached to the front ring gently steers them to the side, redirecting their attention back to you and discouraging the forward momentum.

This harness is constructed from soft, yet durable nylon webbing, ensuring it’s robust enough for daily use with strong dogs. It features four adjustment points – two on the shoulders and two on the chest/girth – allowing for a custom, secure fit for a wide range of body types. It comes in various sizes, with the XL version specifically catering to larger breeds like Mastiffs, Great Danes, Newfoundlands, and other dogs with a chest girth of 34-46 inches and a weight over 80 lbs. ## What Could Be Better

While the [PetSafe Easy Walk No-Pull Dog Harness XL](https://amzn.to/4coIuRk) shines in many areas, no product is perfect. Here are a few points where we think there's room for improvement.

**1. Initial Adjustment Can Be Fiddly:** Getting the perfect fit can be a bit of a puzzle the first few times. With four adjustment points and no padded labeling for which strap goes where (beyond color coding), it requires a bit of trial and error to ensure it’s snug but not too tight, and positioned correctly. The instructions are helpful, but patience is definitely a virtue during the initial setup. If you have a wriggly dog, this step can be a bit of a challenge.

**2. Potential for Leash Tangling:** Because the leash clips to the front of the dog's chest, there's a slight possibility of the leash getting tangled under your dog's front legs, especially if they're particularly energetic or you're using a longer leash. While it's generally a minor inconvenience easily remedied by guiding the leash, it's something to be aware of during walks. It doesn't happen constantly, but it's a more frequent occurrence with front-clip harnesses than with back-clip designs.

**3. Chest Strap Can Rub on Some Body Types:** While generally comfortable, the horizontal chest strap that runs across the dog's chest (just above the legs) can, in some cases, cause minor rubbing or irritation for dogs with very deep chests or unusual body proportions if not perfectly adjusted. This wasn't a universal issue with our test dogs, but it's a common comment we've seen from other owners. It emphasizes the importance of getting that initial fit just right and monitoring your dog for any signs of discomfort, especially during the first few uses.
